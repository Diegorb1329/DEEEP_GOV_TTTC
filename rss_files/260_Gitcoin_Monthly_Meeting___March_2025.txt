    At the start of each month, the Gitcoin team has an hour-long meeting that primarily focuses on strategy and and progress against our goals. This month, we focused on financial updates, sales pipeline review, and GG23.

Link: https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1aXztuZ6_N_JVJkl5pGGVwMbFpouNOG292QnHapvZAQA/edit?usp=sharing

### **Key Topics Discussed:**
1. **Kudos & EthDenver Recap**
   - Shout-out to the team for a successful event.
   - **Schelling Point** was a big branding success:
     - 1,100+ registrations, 500+ attendees, 3,700 livestream viewers.
     - $92K in sponsorships covering most costs.
     - Increased social media engagement and brand visibility.

2. **2025 Funding & Treasury Updates**
   - Decline in token prices impacted Gitcoin‚Äôs financials:
     - Treasury fell from $20M in Dec 2024 to ~$7.2M in March 2025.
     - Additional $4M held in other wallets.
   - Action Plan:
     - Diversifying holdings (ETH & stables).
     - Converting altcoins to stablecoins.
     - Yield optimization through Avant Garde.

3. **Revenue & Profitability**
   - Q1 Performance: Net loss of $595K.
   - Targeting cost reductions and revenue increase.
   - Key pipeline deals:
     - Large $3M deal nearing closure.
     - Several smaller ($30K‚Äì$1M) deals in discussion.

4. **Gitcoin Grants 23 (GG23)**
   - **Community Rounds:** $130K funding for top six projects.
   - **QF & Retro Rounds:** $600K pool for 30 projects.
   - **Onboarding program (March 12-27):** Helps new/existing grantees maximize their impact.

5. **Q1 Progress on 2025 Goals**
   - **Achievements:**
     - Multi-mechanism Gitcoin Grants launched.
     - Builder onboarding program initiated.
   - **Challenges:**
     - Incentive program delayed due to resource constraints.
     - Big enterprise partnership ($3M+/yr) is close but not yet finalized.

### **Next Steps:**
- Continue refining funding strategies.
- Finalize enterprise partnership.
- Improve marketing and community engagement.
- Optimize grant distribution mechanisms.

<p>We had a call today with top stewards by voting ( <a href="https://www.tally.xyz/gov/gitcoin" class="inline-onebox">Tally | Gitcoin</a> ) power to discuss the prospects for GTC and each of its side-daos.</p>

<h3><a name="product-demo-updates-2" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#product-demo-updates-2"></a>Product Demo Updates</h3>
<ul>
<li>Ed presented three product demos:
<ul>
<li>Retrofunding product for GG23 ($600k allocation)
<ul>
<li>Metrics-based approach vs project-based</li>
<li>Admin panel with program/round management</li>
<li>Embedded Checkr AI review tool</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>GTC Staking experiment
<ul>
<li>Will launch with QF voting</li>
<li>Projects with most GTC staked get higher ranking</li>
<li>Staking on Ethereum mainnet due to liquidity</li>
<li>Duration matches round length</li>
<li>Stakers earn rewards based on matching pool ratio</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Contest allocation tools
<ul>
<li>Built for Luxo and Polygon</li>
<li>Leaderboard-style competition format</li>
<li>Tracks metric deltas over 3-4 week periods</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>

<h3><a name="gg23-program-updates-5" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#gg23-program-updates-5"></a>GG23 Program Updates</h3>

<ul>
<li>$1.2M total matching pool</li>
<li>Split equally between QF ($600k) and retro ($600k)</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Retro round focuses on top 30 GG builders from GG18-GG22
<ul>

<li>Strong positive sentiment from builders</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>Record number of community round applications</li>
<li>Operating with lean team (2.5 people)</li>
<li>Exploring further community round decentralization</li>
</ul>

<li>Various sub-projects with different realization probabilities</li>
</ul>
</li>

</ul>
