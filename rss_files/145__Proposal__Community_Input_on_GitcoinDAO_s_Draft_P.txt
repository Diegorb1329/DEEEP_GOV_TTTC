    # Summary

This proposal seeks community input on GitcoinDAO‚Äôs Purpose and Essential Intent to create greater alignment among stakeholders.

**Amended Timeline:** To facilitate additional input from the community, the deadline to provide feedback to the draft purpose and essential intent statements is extended one week to **Friday June 3, 2022**. 

# Context

Earlier this year, several members of CSDO raised concerns that there was growing misalignment between GitcoinDAO contributors, stewards, community, and fans around the DAO's mission and methods for delivering outcomes on that mission.

Some of the confusion is historic. Since Gitcoin launched in 2017, there have been several iterations of its articulated [mission, vision, and values](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/a-visual-history-of-gitcoins-mission-2017-2022/10049). Along the way, followers and contributors alike were drawn in by the company's continuous evolution within the ecosystem, first as a funder of open source, and eventually, as a builder and funder of public goods. Though directionally similar, how community members frame Gitcoin Holdings‚Äô impact is often a signal of when they started following and contributing to the project.

Part of this misalignment is also due to where we are in our organizational development. GitcoinDAO will be a year old this month (yay!). In the midst of the disaffiliation from Gitcoin Holdings, contributors are developing the common rituals, processes, and language that clearly articulate our vision for the world we are trying to build.

It is also increasingly clear that the confusion over the DAO‚Äôs purpose and essential work is beginning to affect how we create alignment with all the constituents we have. This was most recently felt during the S14 budget process where stewards and contributors voiced concerns about the various workstream projects and associated spending, which seemed out of sync with the stated mission and vision of Gitcoin Holdings. The broader market conditions and the desire for tighter alignment underscored the need for a clear statement of purpose, and focused direction on the DAO‚Äôs medium-term goals.

During a recent cross-stream strategy session held on May 6th,, workstream leads and Gitcoin Holdings attendees brainstormed potential statements of GitcoinDAO‚Äôs Purpose and Essential Intent (see [link](https://app.mural.co/t/theready7827/m/theready7827/1651759127362/e725e0a698f03944495507ed206cf5f90bd07428?sender=sam3202)).

# Process to-date

During the strategy session, workstream leads and Gitcoin Holding attendees met with the goal of developing a shared sense of potential statements of DAO-level Purpose and Essential Intent. The session was facilitated by Sam Spurlin of *The Ready*.

Instead of focusing on traditional Mission and Vision statements, Purpose and essential intents seek to offer context on what work matters most within an organization and and why.

![|624x104](upload://ilOiqnPnTcCBrJNcHwKFOPwAvPf.png)

![|624x133](upload://w0u1ZbJZI3nZJQ8DuK5aQey4Gyd.png)

Attendees were asked to consider:

* The definitions of a stated purpose and essential intent (see above).
  * Instead of focusing on what an organization does (a mission statement), a Purpose statement describes the reason it exists.
  * Essential intent describes the concrete, measurable work of contributors to an organization in pursuit of the overall purpose.
* The utility of developing and articulating a DAO-level purpose and essential intents.
* The role of the GitcoinDAO community articulating and consenting to the drafted artifacts.

Using previous Gitcoin discussions on the topic as inspiration, attendees drafted and voted on potential purpose and essential intent statements.

The session ended with the attendees electing a subgroup to synthesize the discoveries of the session into draft language for consideration by the broader community. The subgroup included  @annika, @owocki, @lthrift, @kyle, and @Jodi_GitcoinDAO.

Draft language was presented and discussed during [CSDO Meeting #32](https://www.notion.so/gitcoin/CSDO-Cross-Stream-DAO-Ops-32-48eafb8da28c4daabf105442a035200a) held on May 17th. The group decided we had enough conviction in the working group‚Äôs language that we were ready to move the discussion forward to our community.

### Emergent Themes and Tensions from Session

We are an Impact DAO working to launch our Protocol. We will transition to a Protocol DAO focused on building and funding tools that support community-driven shared resources. We knew we wanted to stay true to our earlier mission and ensure the purpose and essential intents were focused on empowering the builders of funders to use Gitcoin Grants to realize the green pilled future we have imagined.

We agreed the Grants protocol is the most important essential intent for us to focus on over the next 12mos. We also quickly identified the thematic need to ensure we thrive as a DAO ‚Äî financially, in the impact we deliver, and in the example we set for the web3 ecosystem.

You can find more of the details in the session notes, which are documented [here](https://app.mural.co/t/theready7827/m/theready7827/1651759127362/e725e0a698f03944495507ed206cf5f90bd07428?sender=sam3202). The work of the elected subgroup can be found [here](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1UtgdSH5RNLJQ4bxX8irew93hQYoMFp2vKO-alQogPBc/edit?usp=sharing).

# Draft Purpose and Essential Intent

## Purpose

To empower communities to build & fund their shared needs.

## Essential Intents‚Ä¶in no particular order

Grants Protocol (+ Growth)

* Build a widely adopted, modular Grants protocol that creates a flourishing ecosystem of funding mechanisms.

Financial Sustainability

* Have a comprehensive, diversified strategy for financial stability of the DAO in order to effectively achieve our mission.

DAO Organization

* Create and promote clear and engaging rituals, communication channels, and messaging that build shared context among GitcoinDAO‚Äôs contributors, stewards, and supporters.

Grants Program

* Enable a successful grants protocol launch by dogfooding it internally & supporting ecosystems in adopting it.

* Continue to support the Ethereum ecosystem & public goods in web3 and beyond through Gitcoin-led rounds.

## Additional Context

At the same CSDO meeting, workstream leads discussed the following tradeoffs, tensions, and opportunities that emerged from the proposed language, including:

* The potential confusion created by moving from a ‚Äòpublic goods‚Äô or ‚Äòopen source‚Äô framing in our stated purpose to one that centers a community‚Äôs ‚Äòshared needs‚Äô.
* How creating a distinction between the Grants program directed by the PGF and the Grants Protocol allows for flexibility in messaging the importance of each.
* What role our ethics play in informing our purpose and essential work.
* The constraints created by including prescriptive language in our essential Intents.  

Ultimately, we cannot finalize these artifacts without feedback from you on these or other emergent considerations.

# Request to the Community

**We are seeking community comment on the draft purpose and each essential intent by May 27th, 2022** (1 week from today). In parallel, we will be presenting the draft language at a Steward Council call to be held on Tuesday May 24th to seek their advice and/or consent.

It‚Äôs important to conclude this post noting that the goal of this proposal is to build in the open. We hope that this process helps us work with clarity and focus over the the 12-18 months, and well into the future. Be on the lookout for a post from our friends at MMM with updates on how some of the work in this post ties into the DAO‚Äôs brand strategy.

We appreciate your engagement with this proposal in the comment thread below.

Sincerely,

CSDO

            <p>As promised, adding here the flow and takeaways from our <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/the-steward-council-formation-mandate/9843">Stewards Council</a> session on Tuesday. The aims for the session were as follows:</p>
<ul>
<li>The council understanding the process through which Purpose and Essential Intent was developed and the reasons it felt necessary at this time in the evolution of this DAO</li>
<li>The council having an opportunity to share meaningful feedback on the Purpose and Essential Intent and continuing to evolve it into something useful &amp; conducive to this DAO thriving</li>
</ul>
<p>We specifically split the P&amp;EI portion of the call in two stages and ran the session in Mural:</p>
<ul>
<li>Record reactions, suggestions, and questions (via individual private mode brainstorm to enable unhindered honesty)</li>
<li>Have an open conversation about Purpose &amp; Essential Intent based on the recorded questions and reactions</li>
</ul>

</blockquote>
</aside>
<p>Would love to weigh in here. IMO it‚Äôs not essential that the purpose is something that is directly meme-able. Most of the time, organizations do not market (here I mostly mean ‚Äúamplify‚Äù) their purpose directly. Instead, they come up other artifacts that do this for them - like a tag line. Or audience-customized versions of these. While I agree that ‚Äúshared needs‚Äù is not very meme-able, I don‚Äôt think that this is a requirement for a purpose, which is primarily for internal use in my view.</p>
<p>That being said, I‚Äôve crafted a vision &amp; a mission as well based on the purpose being discussed. Perhaps these are more ‚Äúopinionated‚Äù and therefore more inspiring.</p>
<p>First, some definitions. Purpose, Vision, Mission &amp; Impact can all sound somewhat similar. Here‚Äôs how we can tell them apart.</p>
<ol>
<li>Purpose guides you.</li>
<li>Mission drives you.</li>
<li>Vision is where you aspire to be.</li>
<li>Impact is what matters.</li>
</ol>
<p><em><strong>Gitcoin‚Äôs purpose is to empower communities to build &amp; fund their shared needs.</strong></em><br>
^ This is what ‚Äúguides‚Äù us, not what drives us. (This is what we‚Äôre ratifying in the orig post.)</p>
<p><em><strong>Gitcoin‚Äôs mission is to help ecosystems grow &amp; thrive by building protocols &amp; programs that enable them to build &amp; fund their shared needs.</strong></em> (my draft version)<br>
^ Is this what drives us? It‚Äôs more narrow &amp; opinionated than the purpose. (We could potentially add a qualifier to ecosystems such as ‚Äúpositive sum‚Äù if we want to be more selective in the ecosystems we support with our limited resources.)</p>
<p><em><strong>Gitcoin‚Äôs vision is to create a world where millions of diverse, interdependent, cooperative &amp; autonomous communities flourish, thereby enabling universal human thriving.</strong></em> (my draft version)<br>
^ Is this what we aspire to create? Are these the right adjectives? Words matter!</p>
<p>Finally, our impact should be quantified and outlined. What we have outlined with essential intent  here are close but IMO none are not nearly measurable enough.</p>
<p>Let‚Äôs take a look at the graphic <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/owocki">@owocki</a> shared:<br>
</p><div class="lightbox-wrapper"><a class="lightbox" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/original/2X/c/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3.jpeg" data-download-href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3" title="image"><img src="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/c/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3_2_517x287.jpeg" alt="image" data-base62-sha1="tgGkf7lFFqlYmlghZMDeOa3TjvZ" width="517" height="287" srcset="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/c/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3_2_517x287.jpeg, https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/c/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3_2_775x430.jpeg 1.5x, https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/c/cd21d040fc12a18c846b9ea3e4043061b7612fc3_2_1034x574.jpeg 2x" data-dominant-color="110A35"></a></div><p></p>
<p>We could adopt the following north star metrics (or something like it):<br>
<em>By the end of 2023, we have 69 communities that have adopted our protocols, enabling at least 69420 individual grants, funded by 6,942,000 contributors.</em></p>
<p>Imagine how the above vision, purpose, mission, and impact would help us to prioritize our daily, weekly, seasonal work and keep us aligned across workstreams?</p>
<p><strong>How has our mission changed over time?</strong><br>
2017: 		Push open source forward. Gitcoin is the easiest way to monetize or incentivize work in Open Source Software<br>
2019: 		Grow &amp; sustain open source.<br>
2021: 		Build &amp; fund digital public goods.<br>
End of 2021: 	Gitcoin DAO builds for human thriving (not fully adopted)<br>
<strong>Now:</strong> 		<strong>Help ecosystems grow &amp; thrive</strong> <em>(shortened version of the above draft mission)</em></p>
<p><strong>What type of DAO are we?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>We are a ‚Äúprotocol / impact‚Äù DAO - a DAO that designs, builds &amp; deploys open protocols that create positive externalities. (Q: Is there a better name for this? I‚Äôm more focused on the definition here, which is below):
<ul>
<li>How do we measure success? We measure our success across both utility &amp; impact.</li>
<li>Today, BOTH utility &amp; impact are reflected in the Gitcoin DAO governance token (GTC). (Is that sustainable &amp; scalable?)</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>What are we not?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>We are not a grants DAO</li>
<li>We are not a protocol DAO ‚Üê a pure protocol DAO measures its value only in utility</li>
<li>We are not a social DAO ‚Üê while we have many social events, these are in service of our broader purpose to empower communities to build &amp; fund their shared needs</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>How do we think about our journey?</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>We launched a number of experiments to support open source builders &amp; open source ecosystems - bounties, kernel, grants</li>
<li>The most successful mechanism was grants. We experimented with grants and grants rounds as a way to allocate funds to promising projects and builders. QF was the mechanism that really found PMF because of the way it harnesses voices from the community.</li>
<li>We helped many incredible projects get off the ground directly by raising money from matching pool sponsors &amp; pairing them with community donations through quarterly grants rounds &amp; QF</li>
<li>We then expanded to offer grants rounds as a service to organizations like Uniswap that are looking to efficiently allocate funds to grow their ecosystems</li>
<li>The product-market fit for ecosystem rounds was so immediate and clear that we‚Äôre now decentralizing our monolithic grants product into composable grants money legos. This will allow for supercharged adoption &amp; impact.</li>
<li>We will start to deemphasize running grants rounds directly and emphasize building &amp; scaling protocols that allow web3 projects to run their own grants rounds.</li>
<li>This pivot led to the reevaluation of our mission, purpose, vision and intended impact. We go from DAO-to-individual as our primary go-to-market to a primarily DAO-to-DAO model over the coming seasons.</li>
</ul>
<p>Would love thoughts on any/all of the above ways to describe our evolution.</p>
<p><strong>Why is this important for marketing?</strong><br>
Our vision, purpose, mission &amp; impact feed into our overall marketing strategy. They are what is used to create a channel &amp; content strategy. To amplify our key messages across all of our channels - websites, Twitter, Discord, YouTube, etc. With this clarity, we can much more easily spin up new channels that all align too. We will use this clarity to  speak with a unified voice to all of our audiences, thereby increasing awareness, comprehension &amp; action.</p>
<p>Please reach out to me directly if you have thoughts here!</p>
<p>And know that we‚Äôre working on other ways to engage with the community on these topics outside of this gov forum as well!</p>

<p>I have 3 thots on this:</p>
<h1><a name="h-1-1" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#h-1-1"></a>1</h1>
<p>I think that one implication of becoming a protocol dao is to be <a href="https://nakamoto.com/credible-neutrality/">credibly neutral</a>, defined as follows:  <em>Essentially, a mechanism is credibly neutral if just by looking at the mechanism‚Äôs design, it is easy to see that the mechanism does not discriminate for or against any specific people</em></p>
<p>The great benefit of a protocol is that GitcoinDAO <a href="https://vitalik.ca/general/2019/05/09/control_as_liability.html">does not have control nor liability</a>.  This means there is no intermediary who can rent seek, nor is there an intermediary who can discriminate against specific people.  But there is also no one to impose Gitcoin‚Äôs values (beyond what is baked into the protocol itself - IMO QF is already an opinionated mechanism).</p>
<h1><a name="h-2-2" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#h-2-2"></a>2</h1>
<p>Here is how I think about public goods vs other types of goods.<br>
<img src="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/original/2X/e/e7e7af439f231a02c1f25969ad7b19c5f8923d59.jpeg" alt="goods" data-base62-sha1="x5wG13D6pUr8tFKod9gM5DzZUbD" width="680" height="318"></p>
<p>We put ‚Äòshared needs‚Äô in the purpose because we didnt want to continue getting drawn into long technocratic discussions defining public goods (ask the FDD about what its like to be in the middle of one of these - its a very large time sink + not always very fruitful in a world where there is much nuance between public goods + other types of goods).</p>
<p>We felt that it was more important to meet the shared needs of the communities we serve, and in a world where public goods are the most underfunded, the communities will naturally be drawn via the protocol to allocate resources there.</p>
<h1><a name="h-3-3" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#h-3-3"></a>3</h1>
<p>I dont worry about the product being used for nefarious purposes, because Quadratic Funding naturally optimizes for broadly what communities want, and I believe that people in communities very often want positive things for themselves - and very rarely do people broadly advertise doing nefarious/immoral things.  Bc QF optimizes for the preferences of the broad many, I think the QF mechanism is resistent to this concern.</p>

Jodi_GitcoinDAO

            <h1>
<a name="summary-1" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#summary-1"></a>Summary</h1>
<p>This proposal seeks community input on GitcoinDAO‚Äôs Purpose and Essential Intent to create greater alignment among stakeholders.</p>
<p><strong>Amended Timeline:</strong> To facilitate additional input from the community, the deadline to provide feedback to the draft purpose and essential intent statements is extended one week to <strong>Friday June 3, 2022</strong>.</p>
<h1>
<a name="context-2" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#context-2"></a>Context</h1>
<p>Earlier this year, several members of CSDO raised concerns that there was growing misalignment between GitcoinDAO contributors, stewards, community, and fans around the DAO‚Äôs mission and methods for delivering outcomes on that mission.</p>
<p>Some of the confusion is historic. Since Gitcoin launched in 2017, there have been several iterations of its articulated <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/a-visual-history-of-gitcoins-mission-2017-2022/10049">mission, vision, and values</a>. Along the way, followers and contributors alike were drawn in by the company‚Äôs continuous evolution within the ecosystem, first as a funder of open source, and eventually, as a builder and funder of public goods. Though directionally similar, how community members frame Gitcoin Holdings‚Äô impact is often a signal of when they started following and contributing to the project.</p>
<p>Part of this misalignment is also due to where we are in our organizational development. GitcoinDAO will be a year old this month (yay!). In the midst of the disaffiliation from Gitcoin Holdings, contributors are developing the common rituals, processes, and language that clearly articulate our vision for the world we are trying to build.</p>
<p>It is also increasingly clear that the confusion over the DAO‚Äôs purpose and essential work is beginning to affect how we create alignment with all the constituents we have. This was most recently felt during the S14 budget process where stewards and contributors voiced concerns about the various workstream projects and associated spending, which seemed out of sync with the stated mission and vision of Gitcoin Holdings. The broader market conditions and the desire for tighter alignment underscored the need for a clear statement of purpose, and focused direction on the DAO‚Äôs medium-term goals.</p>
<p>During a recent cross-stream strategy session held on May 6th, workstream leads and Gitcoin Holdings attendees brainstormed potential statements of GitcoinDAO‚Äôs Purpose and Essential Intent (see <a href="https://app.mural.co/t/theready7827/m/theready7827/1651759127362/e725e0a698f03944495507ed206cf5f90bd07428?sender=sam3202" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">link</a>).</p>
<h1>
<a name="process-to-date-3" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#process-to-date-3"></a>Process to-date</h1>
<p>During the strategy session, workstream leads and Gitcoin Holding attendees met with the goal of developing a shared sense of potential statements of DAO-level Purpose and Essential Intent. The session was facilitated by Sam Spurlin of <em>The Ready</em>.</p>
<p>Instead of focusing on traditional Mission and Vision statements, Purpose and essential intents seek to offer context on what work matters most within an organization and and why.</p>
<p></p><div class="lightbox-wrapper"><a class="lightbox" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/original/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265.png" data-download-href="/uploads/short-url/ilOiqnPnTcCBrJNcHwKFOPwAvPf.png?dl=1" title="" rel="noopener nofollow ugc"><img src="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265_2_624x104.png" alt="" data-base62-sha1="ilOiqnPnTcCBrJNcHwKFOPwAvPf" width="624" height="104" srcset="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265_2_624x104.png, /uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265_2_936x156.png 1.5x, /uploads/db4391/original/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265.png 2x" data-small-upload="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/8/809e4fc2e199cc18a6b372257d73aa8ff8cc3265_2_10x10.png"></a></div><p></p>
<p></p><div class="lightbox-wrapper"><a class="lightbox" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/uploads/db4391/original/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431.png" data-download-href="/uploads/short-url/w0u1ZbJZI3nZJQ8DuK5aQey4Gyd.png?dl=1" title="" rel="noopener nofollow ugc"><img src="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431_2_624x133.png" alt="" data-base62-sha1="w0u1ZbJZI3nZJQ8DuK5aQey4Gyd" width="624" height="133" srcset="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431_2_624x133.png, /uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431_2_936x199.png 1.5x, /uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431_2_1248x266.png 2x" data-small-upload="/uploads/db4391/optimized/2X/e/e0539598a757dae6c4481ef82ee32db27888e431_2_10x10.png"></a></div><p></p>
<p>Attendees were asked to consider:</p>
<ul>
<li>The definitions of a stated purpose and essential intent (see above).
<ul>
<li>Instead of focusing on what an organization does (a mission statement), a Purpose statement describes the reason it exists.</li>
<li>Essential intent describes the concrete, measurable work of contributors to an organization in pursuit of the overall purpose.</li>
</ul>
</li>
<li>The utility of developing and articulating a DAO-level purpose and essential intents.</li>
<li>The role of the GitcoinDAO community articulating and consenting to the drafted artifacts.</li>
</ul>
<p>Using previous Gitcoin discussions on the topic as inspiration, attendees drafted and voted on potential purpose and essential intent statements.</p>
<p>The session ended with the attendees electing a subgroup to synthesize the discoveries of the session into draft language for consideration by the broader community. The subgroup included  <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/annika">@annika</a>, <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/owocki">@owocki</a>, <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/lthrift">@lthrift</a>, <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/kyle">@kyle</a>, and <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/jodi_gitcoindao">@Jodi_GitcoinDAO</a>.</p>
<p>Draft language was presented and discussed during <a href="https://www.notion.so/gitcoin/CSDO-Cross-Stream-DAO-Ops-32-48eafb8da28c4daabf105442a035200a" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">CSDO Meeting #32</a> held on May 17th. The group decided we had enough conviction in the working group‚Äôs language that we were ready to move the discussion forward to our community.</p>
<h3>
<a name="emergent-themes-and-tensions-from-session-4" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#emergent-themes-and-tensions-from-session-4"></a>Emergent Themes and Tensions from Session</h3>
<p>We are an Impact DAO working to launch our Protocol. We will transition to a Protocol DAO focused on building and funding tools that support community-driven shared resources. We knew we wanted to stay true to our earlier mission and ensure the purpose and essential intents were focused on empowering the builders of funders to use Gitcoin Grants to realize the green pilled future we have imagined.</p>
<p>We agreed the Grants protocol is the most important essential intent for us to focus on over the next 12mos. We also quickly identified the thematic need to ensure we thrive as a DAO ‚Äî financially, in the impact we deliver, and in the example we set for the web3 ecosystem.</p>
<p>You can find more of the details in the session notes, which are documented <a href="https://app.mural.co/t/theready7827/m/theready7827/1651759127362/e725e0a698f03944495507ed206cf5f90bd07428?sender=sam3202" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">here</a>. The work of the elected subgroup can be found <a href="https://docs.google.com/document/d/1UtgdSH5RNLJQ4bxX8irew93hQYoMFp2vKO-alQogPBc/edit?usp=sharing" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">here</a>.</p>
<h1>
<a name="draft-purpose-and-essential-intent-5" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#draft-purpose-and-essential-intent-5"></a>Draft Purpose and Essential Intent</h1>
<h2>
<a name="purpose-6" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#purpose-6"></a>Purpose</h2>
<p>To empower communities to build &amp; fund their shared needs.</p>
<h2>
<a name="essential-intentsin-no-particular-order-7" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#essential-intentsin-no-particular-order-7"></a>Essential Intents‚Ä¶in no particular order</h2>
<p>Grants Protocol (+ Growth)</p>
<ul>
<li>Build a widely adopted, modular Grants protocol that creates a flourishing ecosystem of funding mechanisms.</li>
</ul>
<p>Financial Sustainability</p>
<ul>
<li>Have a comprehensive, diversified strategy for financial stability of the DAO in order to effectively achieve our mission.</li>
</ul>
<p>DAO Organization</p>
<ul>
<li>Create and promote clear and engaging rituals, communication channels, and messaging that build shared context among GitcoinDAO‚Äôs contributors, stewards, and supporters.</li>
</ul>
<p>Grants Program</p>
<ul>
<li>
<p>Enable a successful grants protocol launch by dogfooding it internally &amp; supporting ecosystems in adopting it.</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Continue to support the Ethereum ecosystem &amp; public goods in web3 and beyond through Gitcoin-led rounds.</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h2>
<a name="additional-context-8" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#additional-context-8"></a>Additional Context</h2>
<p>At the same CSDO meeting, workstream leads discussed the following tradeoffs, tensions, and opportunities that emerged from the proposed language, including:</p>
<ul>
<li>The potential confusion created by moving from a ‚Äòpublic goods‚Äô or ‚Äòopen source‚Äô framing in our stated purpose to one that centers a community‚Äôs ‚Äòshared needs‚Äô.</li>
<li>How creating a distinction between the Grants program directed by the PGF and the Grants Protocol allows for flexibility in messaging the importance of each.</li>
<li>What role our ethics play in informing our purpose and essential work.</li>
<li>The constraints created by including prescriptive language in our essential Intents.</li>
</ul>
<p>Ultimately, we cannot finalize these artifacts without feedback from you on these or other emergent considerations.</p>
<h1>
<a name="request-to-the-community-9" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#request-to-the-community-9"></a>Request to the Community</h1>
