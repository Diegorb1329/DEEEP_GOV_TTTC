    # KERNEL: A custom educational web

This proposal seeks to establish a GitcoinDAO workstream for [KERNEL](http://kernel.community/), a custom web3 educational institution, built carefully to create the conditions to encourage better humans, building a better web. It is a transformative educational experience in web3.

## AMENDED TL;DR x2
KERNEL seeks an amended **49K in GTC for Season 14** (a 67% reduction from our original request of 150K GTC), our first as a GitcoinDAO workstream. KERNEL is spinning out from Gitcoin Holdings with ~$100K USDC in treasury, after two years building KERNEL within Gitcoin Holdings.

KERNEL is a unique experience for both [web3 newcomers and web3 veterans](https://www.kernel.community/en/#meet-the-kernel-fellows), allowing KERNEL to guide talented contributors across the web3 ecosystem, whether that is GitcoinDAO, other protocols, or stewarding their own projects. KERNEL ultimately hopes to be an educational institution that is a public good, creating positive externalities for web3, first and foremost, by having a positive impact on the lives of KERNEL Fellows and the communities they are responsible for.

These communities are wide-ranging, including [a variety of high potential web3 protocols, projects, DAO's, and other adventures](https://www.kernel.community/en/#projects--teams). Already, seven early stage KERNEL projects are slated to give .5% of tokens/equity to the KERNEL treasury during S14, with a potential for 15 more during S15 and S16. We still have many blocks to come.

Because Gitcoin Holdings already holds 24% of ‚Äòteam governance rights‚Äô in KERNEL, and hopes to transition this to the DAO, we think this will serve GitcoinDAO well over time as we collaborate to build a public institution for all.

**Our intention is to raise funding for KERNEL in a completely web3-native way (directly from protocols, no venture funding), starting at home with GitcoinDAO.** We took inspiration from [Nomic Labs](https://gov.uniswap.org/t/consensus-check-should-uniswap-governance-contribute-funding-to-the-nomic-foundation/16395), who recently completed a similar fundraise for Hardhat with $2MM in anchor support from the Ethereum Foundation and support from Uniswap, Optimism, Aave, and others for a total $30MM fundraise.

We hope GitcoinDAO will consider being KERNEL‚Äôs anchor partner. This (shortened and trimmed) proposal describes a bit about our last two years, but focuses on our use of 49K GTC during S14 and our intentions as a GitcoinDAO workstream for S14 - S16.

You can see the original proposal for 150K GTC, which has been now reduced by 67%, by starting [here](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/s14-proposal-kernel-a-gitcoin-workstream-proposal/10444?u=mittal#kernel-as-public-good-2). The section that immediately follows will focus on the amendments to the original proposal, including a brief update to milestones and the thinner budget for S14.

GitcoinDAO is a schelling point for creators and funders of digital public goods who want to build the common architecture that will allow hundreds of DAOs to succeed in non-extractive, collaborative and complementary ways. 

KERNEL aims to be a home for many of those creators. This all means that we value GitcoinDAO as a space where we can safely explore what it means for KERNEL to be a public good.

In providing 49K GTC, and potentially supporting across 2-3 seasons, GitcoinDAO provides the seed in an imperfect garden of giving which may last a generation.

# === The original KERNEL S14 proposal: Comments below === 

**tl;dr:** This proposal seeks to establish a GitcoinDAO workstream for [KERNEL](http://kernel.community/), a custom web3 educational institution, built carefully to create the conditions to encourage better humans, building a better web. **We seek 150K in GTC for Season 14.** 

After two years within Gitcoin Holdings, KERNEL is seeking $5mm in total funding from the ecosystem as a part of its transition to a KERNEL Foundation (a Cayman Foundation Company). 

GitcoinDAO is our intended anchor co-conspirator, and we would like to join as a workstream for ~3 seasons, S14 - S16. Separate from GitcoinDAO, we intend to explore $2-3mm contributions with a variety of other value-aligned ecosystems within web3, open source, and public goods communities to achieve our fundraising goal.

Starting with Genesis Block in May 2020, KERNEL has run five KERNEL blocks to much celebration internally and acclaim externally, alongside 1,200 KERNEL fellows building 200+ web3 projects (more [here](https://www.kernel.community/en/#meet-the-kernel-fellows)).

Along with the steady drumbeat of running KERNEL Blocks, this workstream intends to create a variety of financial, communal, educational and artistic tools for ‚Äòcommunities of care‚Äô, which we hold to be amongst the most significant public goods web3 can create. 

Our intention is to [establish an infinite game](https://www.kernel.community/en/build/games/infinite#to-infinity-and-beyond) which would allow KERNEL to be healthily self-sustaining into perpetuity within a few short years, to the benefit of GitcoinDAO, KERNEL, and hopefully, humans interested in a more caring internet. 

We first describe KERNEL, then share a roadmap for this infinite game, use of funds, and parting thoughts from the KERNEL Syllabus.

## KERNEL as Public Good

Any meaningful shift, within self or society, begins with how we learn and teach.

Valuable knowledge may present itself for free, but it must be given attention, intention, and reflection in order to become personally meaningful. It is this paradox which makes educational gifts the seed around which a community crafting new value models can flourish: ‚Äúa single flower blooms, and throughout the world it is spring‚Äù.

Enter [KERNEL](https://kernel.community/): a trust space for talented individuals to use Web 3 as a means to explore their humanity more holistically. KERNEL begins with a KERNEL ‚ÄúBlock‚Äù: an 8 week experience, anchored by 16 lessons which is filled by 250 interested, talented, and diverse peers.

The topics in a KERNEL block transcend web3 - starting with trust, moving through meaning and value, and eventually ending in giving and scale-ability.

These (free and open-source) lessons provide fertile ground for an eight week experience which is ultimately co-created by the KERNEL Fellows who interact with 1) the content and 2) each other. A deepening of personal spirit and the relationships KERNEL Fellows form with each are the primary orientations of the program.

KERNEL aims to be a living example of what it means to live well together. The experience provides questions, not answers, as we explore: how can we build better lives for ourselves as we build a better web for us all?

KERNEL Fellows are builders at leading web3 companies as founders, dancers, technologists, gamers, economists, designers, artists, and musicians. They deeply enjoy each others company and transcend these distinctions every time we meet them again.

## What is KERNEL‚Äôs most fertile path? 

*‚ÄúThere are at least two kinds of games. One could be called finite, the other infinite. A finite game is played for the purpose of winning, an infinite game for the purpose of continuing the play.‚Äù*

*"The rules of an infinite game must change in the course of play. The rules are changed when the players of an infinite game agree that the play is imperiled by a finite outcome ‚Äî that is, by the victory of some players and the defeat of others. The rules of an infinite game are changed to prevent anyone from winning the game and to bring as many persons as possible into the play."*

 -Dr. James P. Carse, [Finite & Infinite Games](https://www.kernel.community/en/build/games/infinite/) 

150K in GTC prepares us for our next act -- establishing KERNEL as an "infinite" pattern for communities of care by: 

- Running KERNEL blocks ad infinitum
- Establishing a perpetual, web3 native funding model for KERNEL
- Open-sourcing peer-to-peer educational tools & patterns
- Creating meaningful, timeless web3 artifacts 

Each of these share a fundamental "infinite", or "perpetual" characteristic: they avoid short-term outcomes, unhealthy competition, and aim to grow healthier with time.

### Funding The Infinite KERNEL
While our approach to growing community and seeding educational spaces also lean upon infinite game philosophy, we outline below how KERNEL uses it to plan for a healthy financial future for KERNEL and to provide value to GitcoinDAO.

Our first step towards a sustainable, regenerative KERNEL are setting up 1) a public KERNEL Treasury and exploring 2) a KERNEL Endowment.

The KERNEL Treasury will begin with $50K in tokens KERNEL has raised thus far through past KERNEL Blocks and an influx of $100K USDC from Gitcoin Holdings, upon spinout. It would grow substantially if our intended $5mm grant raise from GitcoinDAO and other value-aligned protocols is successful, beginning with this workstream.

However, over the next five years, we expect the KERNEL Treasury will grow the most via KERNEL Adventures. 

Through the generosity of an initial seven initial KERNEL Adventures who have committed between .5% - 1% of equity or tokens to the KERNEL treasury, our eyes open to the possibility that a KERNEL Treasury can be a continuous stream to draw upon for KERNEL into perpetuity, if given time to grow.

In Phase 1, we aim to use the $5mm in commitments from GitcoinDAO and other leading web3 ecosystems, alongside less liquid tokens from KERNEL Alumni, to give ourselves that time.

In Phase 2, we will consider establishing a public KERNEL endowment. Endowments have had notable success in co-creating some of the strongest public infrastructure in the world, both physically and financially. They are [perpetual](https://www.utimco.org/funds-managed/#:~:text=Endowment%20funds%20are%20a%20permanent,support%20to%20both%20in%20perpetuity.) in nature, "managed with consideration given to current and future beneficiaries with the goal of providing the same level of economic support to both in perpetuity."

Our expectation is this workstream will lead us to answers on exactly how this might support KERNEL‚Äôs efforts into perpetuity within a new, web3 context. 

The effort from here then, for KERNEL, is a simple life‚Äôs work: give the gift back.

## Why GitcoinDAO
We choose to make this proposal to GitcoinDAO for a number of reasons. KERNEL was originally created and built within Gitcoin Holdings, which means we have strong ties to Gitcoin, both socially and structurally.

* I (Vivek Singh) served as COO of Gitcoin Holdings for 3 years (2019-2021), and KERNEL was originally constructed in May 2020 at Gitcoin as we looked to find a home for the best and brightest people we met through our work - across hackathons, Gitcoin Grants rounds, and beyond.
* Sachin Mittal (co-founder of KERNEL) and Angela Gilhotra also were employed by Gitcoin Holdings, in roles centered around developer advocacy and both co-created KERNEL
* @cryptowanderer and @sidcode are stewards at GitcoinDAO. Andy expressed [his interest](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-stewards-governance/41/18) in May 2021 and Sid has been involved in the day-to-day operations of the DAO as a tech lead for the MMM workstream for 8+ months now and has contributed across PGF, FDD, and MC in various capacities. We've worked closely with various efforts of the DAO (Gitcoin Grants Rounds, Public Goods are Good, Memepalooza, and more).
* Gitcoin Holdings, as a part of incubating and spinning KERNEL out into the ecosystem, holds an ongoing voice in KERNEL's governance and would receive 24% of 'team governance rights' if there was a token distribution. The intention is for these rights to move to GitcoinDAO over time, per conversations with @owocki and Gitcoin Holdings leadership

A more expansive lens is that GitcoinDAO is a schelling point for creators and funders of digital public goods who want to build the common architecture that will allow hundreds of DAOs to succeed in non-extractive, collaborative and complementary ways. KERNEL aims to be a homebase for many of those creators. This all means that we value GitcoinDAO as a space where we can safely explore what it means for KERNEL to be a public good.

We look forward to being involved as collaborators in GitcoinDAO via CSDO, in governance proposals, and in sharing our perspective around public goods, educational environments, and coordination tools. 

### KERNEL As Gift

This brings us back, finally, to the core of KERNEL: the giving of gifts.

[Gift-giving](https://kernel.community/en/learn/module-7) has always been the heart of genuine teaching and at the heart of Web 3. Both Satoshi and university endowments play on this same concept.

We're asking for a gift that will both allow us to continue exploring the most valuable ways to think about - and live within - web3, and that the gift may bring you into a[ lively circle of continuous and imperfect gift-giving](https://kernel.community/en/learn/module-7/the-gift).

We are looking for the kind of people who[ understand value](https://kernel.community/en/learn/module-1/value) in similar ways to[ KERNEL Fellows](https://www.kernel.community/en/#kernel), and who want to explore what it means to[ create sacred transactional spaces](https://kernel.community/en/learn/module-7/giving/#sacrifice-scarcity) by extending [human trust](https://kernel.community/en/learn/module-0/trust) within a trustless medium.

            <p>After numerous discussions, I have decided I am against this proposal.</p>
<p>Here‚Äôs why: (I have spoken to Vivek on the phone + articulated most of this to him already.)</p>
<p>KERNELs impact in the market is undeniable, and I value Vivek and Andy‚Äôs friendship, but there are major problems with this proposal (and KERNELs longevity) that need to be taken seriously.</p>
<p>I wrote this post because the dao has responsibilities (1) to manage gitcoins own treasury outflows (2) press for some hard but necessary growth in KERNEL.</p>
<p>Here are some reasons I decided I was against this proposal:</p>
<ol>
<li>It is a signal of weakness in the funding market for KERNEL to come back to Gitcoin for funding after we‚Äôve already incubated &amp; spun it out. KERNEL would be in a stronger position if they had an outside lead for their funding round.</li>
<li>In the past, I‚Äôve made deals with KERNEL leadership where they don‚Äôt follow through on their end of the bargain.  The pattern I‚Äôve seen is that they want something, they make some promises to get it, and when they get what they want, they forget the promises and dont follow through on them.   For this reason, I don‚Äôt take them at their word that they‚Äôll be present in CSDO or any other GitcoinDAO oriented activities (&amp; these are conditions of having a GitcoinDAO workstream).</li>
<li>I still have brain damage from the damage Vivek caused to Gitcoin by being negligent as COO during mid/late 2020.   Vivek focused on KERNEL without prudently transferring his responsibilities over the rest of the Gitcoin portfolio.   In a counterfactual world in which he had been doing the job he had agreed upon - managing the product/teams teams performance, coaching them up, <a href="https://avc.com/2017/01/from-the-archives-turning-your-team/">turning the team</a>, shipping the stuff on our roadmap, and not just ignoring all of these responsibilities and letting the team culture rot - we wouldnt have had such a hard reset for kyle/lindsey/kevin olsen on the product side in 2021 (debt we are STILL paying down). The damage here is easily 5x-15x the financial cost of incubating KERNEL itself.  (maybe even larger if you consider how our market position has deteriorated without a functional product team).  Until I figured out what was going on, KERNEL was like a tapeworm consuming resources its host sorely needed.  Setting its host back 6-9 months in time, and creating endemic cultural rot that would take years to unwind.   I was over this when I thought there was going to be a clean deal to spinout KERNEL, but now that we‚Äôre going to consider ongoing workstream relationship with them I think this history (and the damage it has done to my ability to trust Vivek) needs to be articulated.</li>
<li>We set up the KERNEL spinout from holdings deal to give KERNEL runway.  That deal‚Äôs terms made no mention of further funding from GitcoinDAO. KERNEL is essentially double dipping here by playing the dao and the holdco off each other (telling both that it needs it to fund the next 3-6 months of runway). The amount the holdings company gave KERNEL is enough runway for a team of 1 or 2 for a year, or a team of six for the next couple blocks.</li>
<li>This proposal offers nothing back to GitcoinDAO. It‚Äôs essential a ‚Äúsomething for nothing‚Äù proposal.   I believe that the proposal should be mutually beneficial.</li>
<li>This proposal sets a precedent that Gitcoin will continue to fund KERNEL ad infinitum.</li>
<li>KERNEL is not one of Gitcoin‚Äôs Essential Intents.  In a bear market, the DAO should be especially scrutinizing what it funds.</li>
</ol>
<p>Here is what I think should happen:</p>
<p>Given the option, KERNEL chose independent governance.  Now they need to meet the moment and actually follow through to be independent from Gitcoin.  They need to stop returning the the DAO treasury (especially during scarcity times).</p>
<p>That means:</p>
<ol>
<li>Learn to fundraise outside of Gitcoin/GitcoinDAO.</li>
<li>If KERNEL is going to fundraise from GitcoinDAO stop asking for ridiculous terms ($200k for no mutual benefit).  And show GitcoinDAO you will follow through on what you say you‚Äôre going to do.</li>
<li>Make the hard choices about how much burn KERNEL can handle. KERNEL has a staff of 6, which is huge for a project that doesnt want to raise VC and is pre-revenue and pre-financial sustainability.</li>
</ol>
<p>I understand that this comment may seem harsh.   I value KERNEL, but I think it needs to do some growing up now that its ‚Äúleft the house‚Äù.  I hope that these hard conversations lead to the eventual thriving of a more mature KERNEL.</p>
