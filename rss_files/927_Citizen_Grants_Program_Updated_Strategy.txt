    ## Citizen Grants Program Updated Strategy

After the initial pilot of the Citizen Grants Program, and 3 Citizens Retro rounds, it has become clear that we need to redefine what the program is, what we aim to achieve with it, and set more long term goals and objectives to ensure its success for Gitcoin and our community. After an exercise using our own Grants Canvas, as well as internal discussions, the below is how we plan to move forward with the program.

## TL;DR

* Conclude the Citizens‚Äô Innovate and Forward by the end of July as part of the pilot as scoped in the [original proposal](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-gitcoin-s-citizen-grants-program/17470).
* We are moving towards a selective RFP & Direct Grants, simplifying the process.
* Citizens Retro will spin out of the Citizens Program and spin into Gitcoin Grants. **We will not host a Citizens Retro round in 2024, moving to hosting the next one in Q1 of 2025 alongside GG23.**
* We will update the content on the [gitcoin.co/citizens](http://gitcoin.co/citizens) page to reflect the updated direction of the program.
* Run internal and external retrospectives every 3 months as an ongoing period of reflections to iterate and learn. Here‚Äôs what worked, here‚Äôs what didn‚Äôt, and here‚Äôs how we‚Äôre moving forward. Learning, iterating more actively and regularly, ensuring that the community has their voices heard will be an important part of the future of the program.

### Where We‚Äôve Come From

After running a pilot, the following challenges have been identified in the greater adoption of the program:

**Undefined Problem Spaces:**

* Lack of clear problem definitions within Citizens Innovate leads to confusion and ineffective citizen engagement.
* Need for deliberate focal points to encourage community collaboration on shared challenges.

**Limited Context for Proposal Writing:**

* Insufficient understanding of Gitcoin‚Äôs goals and challenges hinders effective contributions.
* Ongoing education on Gitcoin‚Äôs technology stack, governance, and strategic priorities is necessary.

**‚ÄúFirst Mile‚Äù Barrier for Engagement:**

* The initial engagement process for RFPs and GCPs is cumbersome, deterring participants.
* A more user-friendly interface is needed to facilitate smoother initial interactions.

**Limited Distribution:**

* Limited awareness of the Citizens Forward initiative impacts participation and proposal quality.
* Improved outreach and communication are required to boost community engagement.

**Funding Issues:**

* Imbalance in funding: some DAO Citizens had too much funding, leading to inefficiency (Parkinson‚Äôs Law).
* Others had too little, causing talented contributors to leave for other ecosystems.

### Next Steps:

* Conclude Citizens Innovate and Forward by the end of July as part of the pilot as scoped in the [original proposal](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-gitcoin-s-citizen-grants-program/17470).
* Pay off earned grants (i.e., prorated for completed milestones) to active grantees (Karma, Grants Portal)

## Citizens Grants Program 2.0

The Citizens Grants Program aims to enable citizens to contribute to Gitcoin's mission by fostering community-sourced innovation. The program focuses on growing the usability of Gitcoin's ecosystem, making Gitcoin a leader in the space again, and strengthening the feedback loop between value creation and value capture. Additionally, it seeks to bring competitors closer by encouraging them to build on Allo.

In our move to Gitcoin 2.1 and the re-evaluation of the program, below are the objectives that we plan to achieve in the program moving forward.

## RFP‚Äôs

Owner/Driver: @owocki @deltajuliet 
Consulted: DAO
Informed: @MathildaDV 

*Narrow scope. Standalone and independent of anything else. Scoped by internal Gitcoin contributors.*

We plan to simplify our Request For Proposal strategy, where the Gitcoin team will (on an ongoing basis) post RFP‚Äôs to the Gitcoin Forum and promote it out to our community to attract engagement and builds.

**Execute RFPs selectively using existing templates where there are:**

1. an internal owner(s) accountable for scope, quality, oversight
2. a well-defined scope that can be decoupled with minimal dependencies on internal priorities
3. a known preferred list of at least 1 to 2 teams willing to bid for work

In order for this to be successful, the Gitcoin POC uploading the RFP needs to ensure that there is enough context already built into the RFP, as the lack of this has been a problem space in the past. Teams need to know enough and be well equipped to respond to said RFP.

Retro and learnings on what‚Äôs working and what isn‚Äôt, and iterate from there. Ensure that we have high quality RFP‚Äôs, with good budgets attached, with a very clear scope in order to attract high quality citizens.

            <p>I do support putting the citizens round on pause, it certainly felt like it was going adrift. A retrospective interview with some grantees from the citizens round would be helpful to understand whether it motivated them to contribute more to the ecosystem.</p>
<p>Why was it going adrift? It felt like impact was not equal to profit, since the only eligiblilty to enter the round was <em>some</em> contribution, which can be as meager as a forum post or review. After that, its a level playing field with long time contributors, and you can marshall up support to outrank them with enough motivation. The signal boosting method was a good initiative in the last citizens round and I‚Äôd like to see more of that, along with clear limits to funding based on work done (one forum post impact is gauged at $100, so you can‚Äôt receive more than that in the citizen round).</p>
<p>We should also remember the study where financial penalties resulted in parents being even more late to pick up their child. Reason being, the moral part of not picking up your kid from daycare got overshadowed by the financial element when penalties were introduced. So sometimes people want to just contribute for the sake of it, and financializing parts of it can backfire.</p>

            <h2><a name="citizen-grants-program-updated-strategy-1" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#citizen-grants-program-updated-strategy-1"></a>Citizen Grants Program Updated Strategy</h2>
<p>After the initial pilot of the Citizen Grants Program, and 3 Citizens Retro rounds, it has become clear that we need to redefine what the program is, what we aim to achieve with it, and set more long term goals and objectives to ensure its success for Gitcoin and our community. After an exercise using our own Grants Canvas, as well as internal discussions, the below is how we plan to move forward with the program.</p>
<h2><a name="tldr-2" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#tldr-2"></a>TL;DR</h2>
<ul>
<li>Conclude the Citizens‚Äô Innovate and Forward by the end of July as part of the pilot as scoped in the <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-gitcoin-s-citizen-grants-program/17470">original proposal</a>.</li>
<li>We are moving towards a selective RFP &amp; Direct Grants, simplifying the process.</li>
<li>Citizens Retro will spin out of the Citizens Program and spin into Gitcoin Grants. <strong>We will not host a Citizens Retro round in 2024, moving to hosting the next one in Q1 of 2025 alongside GG23.</strong></li>
<li>We will update the content on the <a href="http://gitcoin.co/citizens" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">gitcoin.co/citizens</a> page to reflect the updated direction of the program.</li>
<li>Run internal and external retrospectives every 3 months as an ongoing period of reflections to iterate and learn. Here‚Äôs what worked, here‚Äôs what didn‚Äôt, and here‚Äôs how we‚Äôre moving forward. Learning, iterating more actively and regularly, ensuring that the community has their voices heard will be an important part of the future of the program.</li>
</ul>

<h3><a name="where-weve-come-from-5" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#where-weve-come-from-5"></a>Where We‚Äôve Come From</h3>
<p>After running a pilot, the following challenges have been identified in the greater adoption of the program:</p>
<p><strong>Undefined Problem Spaces:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Lack of clear problem definitions within Citizens Innovate leads to confusion and ineffective citizen engagement.</li>
<li>Need for deliberate focal points to encourage community collaboration on shared challenges.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Limited Context for Proposal Writing:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Insufficient understanding of Gitcoin‚Äôs goals and challenges hinders effective contributions.</li>
<li>Ongoing education on Gitcoin‚Äôs technology stack, governance, and strategic priorities is necessary.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>‚ÄúFirst Mile‚Äù Barrier for Engagement:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>The initial engagement process for RFPs and GCPs is cumbersome, deterring participants.</li>
<li>A more user-friendly interface is needed to facilitate smoother initial interactions.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Limited Distribution:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Limited awareness of the Citizens Forward initiative impacts participation and proposal quality.</li>
<li>Improved outreach and communication are required to boost community engagement.</li>
</ul>
<p><strong>Funding Issues:</strong></p>
<ul>
<li>Imbalance in funding: some DAO Citizens had too much funding, leading to inefficiency (Parkinson‚Äôs Law).</li>
<li>Others had too little, causing talented contributors to leave for other ecosystems.</li>
</ul>
<h3><a name="next-steps-6" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#next-steps-6"></a>Next Steps:</h3>
<ul>
<li>Conclude Citizens Innovate and Forward by the end of July as part of the pilot as scoped in the <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-gitcoin-s-citizen-grants-program/17470">original proposal</a>.</li>
<li>Pay off earned grants (i.e., prorated for completed milestones) to active grantees (Karma, Grants Portal)</li>
</ul>

<h2><a name="rfps-15" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#rfps-15"></a>RFP‚Äôs</h2>
<p>Owner/Driver: <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/owocki">@owocki</a> <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/deltajuliet">@deltajuliet</a><br>
Consulted: DAO<br>
Informed: <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/mathildadv">@MathildaDV</a></p>
<p><em>Narrow scope. Standalone and independent of anything else. Scoped by internal Gitcoin contributors.</em></p>
<p>We plan to simplify our Request For Proposal strategy, where the Gitcoin team will (on an ongoing basis) post RFP‚Äôs to the Gitcoin Forum and promote it out to our community to attract engagement and builds.</p>
<p><strong>Execute RFPs selectively using existing templates where there are:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>an internal owner(s) accountable for scope, quality, oversight</li>
<li>a well-defined scope that can be decoupled with minimal dependencies on internal priorities</li>
<li>a known preferred list of at least 1 to 2 teams willing to bid for work</li>
</ol>
<p>In order for this to be successful, the Gitcoin POC uploading the RFP needs to ensure that there is enough context already built into the RFP, as the lack of this has been a problem space in the past. Teams need to know enough and be well equipped to respond to said RFP.</p>
<p>Retro and learnings on what‚Äôs working and what isn‚Äôt, and iterate from there. Ensure that we have high quality RFP‚Äôs, with good budgets attached, with a very clear scope in order to attract high quality citizens.</p>

<h2><a name="citizens-retro-17" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#citizens-retro-17"></a>Citizens Retro</h2>
<p>Owner/Driver: <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/mathildadv">@MathildaDV</a><br>
Consulted: <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/owocki">@owocki</a> <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/sov">@Sov</a> <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/sejalrekhan">@sejalrekhan</a></p>
<p>Early 2025, late Q1, within GG23. The Citizens Retro will spin out of the Citizens Grants Program and spin into Gitcoin Grants. We believe that the Retro should align with Gitcoin‚Äôs goals, and unlock a more robust opportunity space for citizens.</p>
<p>We are currently putting our Citizens Retro round on hold. <strong>Citizens Retro <span class="hashtag-raw">#4</span> will not take place as currently scheduled in September, rather kicking off early 2025.</strong> This decision did not come lightly, but we believe it‚Äôs important to ensure that the Retro aligns to our goals and creates more clarity for community members on where their contributions will create the most impact in the Gitcoin Ecosystem.</p>
<p>As part of Retro Round design in EasyRetroPGF, suggestive categories and scope of work should be offered that align with internal priorities ahead of the round. With time, this approach will raise awareness amongst citizens about these priorities.</p>
