# Gitcoin Monthly Team Meeting - December 11

## Meeting Purpose
Review 2024 performance and discuss strategic plans for 2025, including new organizational structures and goals.

## Key Takeaways
* Gitcoin aims to distribute $30M in funding in 2025, a 3x increase from 2024
* New organizational initiatives: GrantsLab to focus on enterprise partnerships, AlloLab to drive developer adoption
* Shifting from "protocol-first" to "product-first" approach for Allo, emphasizing on-chain solutions only when necessary
* 2025 financial goals: $2M in revenue, extending runway


## Topics

### 2024 Performance Review
* Distributed $10.3M in funding, short of $15M goal
* Ran 105 grant rounds across 13 chains, funded 1,719 builders
* New leadership team and organizational structure implemented
* Revenue grew to ~$1M, up from $0 in previous year

### 2025 Goals and Strategy
* Distribute $30M in funding, with $25M through partner programs
* Generate $2M in revenue, roughly doubling 2024 revenue
* Focus on measuring and improving 5x return on grant investments
* Implement multi-mechanism approach for Gitcoin Grants program
* Launch Allo as complementary initiative to GrantsLab

### Financial Outlook
* 2025 projected costs: ~$7.5M (GrantsLab 66%, Citzen Grants 25%, DAO 8%)
* Current runway: ~2.5 years at current burn rate
* Foundation budget secure until end of 2026
* GrantsLab aiming for $2M revenue (70% from services, 17.5% from grants, 12.5% from product experiments)

### Allo Strategy Pivot
* Shift from "protocol-first" to "product-first" approach
* Focus on full-stack apps, on-chain only when needed
* Aim for faster iteration and market responsiveness
* New business unit focused on developer adoption and tools
* Network-first strategy to build community of devs using Allo
* Potential launch of investment fund to support strategic projects
