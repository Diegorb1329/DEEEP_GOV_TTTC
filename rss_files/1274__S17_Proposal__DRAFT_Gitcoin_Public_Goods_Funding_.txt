    # Public Goods Funding S17 Workstream Budget Proposal

Thank you to the whole PGF workstream for their help shaping this proposal draft and all the work put in to push us forward as a DAO in S16. A particular shout out to @Maxwell for all his help shaping this budget.  
 
# Essential Intents

During S17, we will continue to prioritize the following EIs
* EI1: Protocol Adoption
* EI2: Financial Sustainability
* EI3: DAO Organization
* EI4: Grants Program Success


# TL;DR

Our S16 goals were: 
* Launch Gitcoin Protocol Alpha Round Successfully
* Transition 90% of selected & willing grantees successfully for Alpha round to the protocol
* Transition Partners successfully to the protocol
* Determine future of the program + services strategy + structure
* Plan Partner + community retreat in support of protocol launch

PGF has remained on track to hit these goals. The two caveats are that (1) we‚Äôre still aligning on details of the services strategy and communicating that more clearly to the broader community (2) we haven‚Äôt fully scheduled and transitioned partners to the protocol. That said, we are in conversation with partners about scheduling rounds from March onward after we launch the beta of Allo Protocol and start testing a fee for service model. We are also in discussions about running featured rounds in April when we launch the next iteration of the Gitcoin Program during our Beta period.

> We‚Äôd also like to celebrate behind the scenes work that exceeded our goals. We launched the UNICEF Innovation round in Q4 2022 that raised over 130 ETH (50 ETH from UNICEF Innovation and 80 ETH from 15,500 Donors)!!



In addition, a less tangible highlight is how the PGF team has become a tight and highly efficient unit. It wasn‚Äôt easy to reorg the team but we‚Äôre down a total of 50% of contributors over the past 3 seasons to 9 FT and 2 PT contributors and one contributor on leave (@annika we‚Äôre excited to have you back soon). We‚Äôre building a strong culture of talented, highly effective, and dedicated contributors.

Our main goals for S17 are twofold: 
**Protocol growth readiness and Gitcoin Grants Program readiness.**

**Our overarching priority in S17 is to continue setting up the protocol for successful adoption with solid infrastructure to drive sustainable growth.**  

Overall we will
1.	Developing runbooks and guides for communities to use to run QF rounds and drive adoption
2.	Running test rounds with partners to onboard and enable them to manage their own programs permissionlessly
3.	Defining and launching the Core Gitcoin Grants Program in April, to embrace our DAO's goal of funding public goods with our protocol
4.	Defining and providing a paid services offering to early adopters until a fully self serve approach is viable



# Amount

PGF's total request will be **$606,000 USD** for S17. A 30% decrease from last season. Accounting for 60+ day reserves and current reserves we are requesting **$337,903 USD** for S17. A 68% decrease from last season. 


***1)** The amount of GTC requested and the value of the reserves will be adjusted based on the current market value at the time this proposal is moved to Tally using the lower of the current price or the 20 day moving average, whichever is lower.*

***2)** The Treasury Request amount is an estimate using the price of $2.10 GTC to calculate the total of the reserves rolled over from Season 16.*

            <h1><a name="public-goods-funding-s17-workstream-budget-proposal-1" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#public-goods-funding-s17-workstream-budget-proposal-1"></a>Public Goods Funding S17 Workstream Budget Proposal</h1>
<p>Thank you to the whole PGF workstream for their help shaping this proposal draft and all the work put in to push us forward as a DAO in S16. A particular shout out to <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/maxwell">@Maxwell</a> for all his help shaping this budget.</p>
<h1><a name="essential-intents-2" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#essential-intents-2"></a>Essential Intents</h1>
<p>During S17, we will continue to prioritize the following EIs</p>
<ul>
<li>EI1: Protocol Adoption</li>
<li>EI2: Financial Sustainability</li>
<li>EI3: DAO Organization</li>
<li>EI4: Grants Program Success</li>
</ul>
<h1><a name="tldr-3" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#tldr-3"></a>TL;DR</h1>
<p>Our S16 goals were:</p>
<ul>
<li>Launch Gitcoin Protocol Alpha Round Successfully</li>
<li>Transition 90% of selected &amp; willing grantees successfully for Alpha round to the protocol</li>
<li>Transition Partners successfully to the protocol</li>
<li>Determine future of the program + services strategy + structure</li>
<li>Plan Partner + community retreat in support of protocol launch</li>
</ul>
<p>PGF has remained on track to hit these goals. The two caveats are that (1) we‚Äôre still aligning on details of the services strategy and communicating that more clearly to the broader community (2) we haven‚Äôt fully scheduled and transitioned partners to the protocol. That said, we are in conversation with partners about scheduling rounds from March onward after we launch the beta of Allo Protocol and start testing a fee for service model. We are also in discussions about running featured rounds in April when we launch the next iteration of the Gitcoin Program during our Beta period.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>We‚Äôd also like to celebrate behind the scenes work that exceeded our goals. We launched the UNICEF Innovation round in Q4 2022 that raised over 130 ETH (50 ETH from UNICEF Innovation and 80 ETH from 15,500 Donors)!!</p>
</blockquote>
<p>In addition, a less tangible highlight is how the PGF team has become a tight and highly efficient unit. It wasn‚Äôt easy to reorg the team but we‚Äôre down a total of 50% of contributors over the past 3 seasons to 9 FT and 2 PT contributors and one contributor on leave (<a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/annika">@annika</a> we‚Äôre excited to have you back soon). We‚Äôre building a strong culture of talented, highly effective, and dedicated contributors.</p>
<p>Our main goals for S17 are twofold:<br>
<strong>Protocol growth readiness and Gitcoin Grants Program readiness.</strong></p>
<p><strong>Our overarching priority in S17 is to continue setting up the protocol for successful adoption with solid infrastructure to drive sustainable growth.</strong></p>
<p>Overall we will</p>
<ol>
<li>Developing runbooks and guides for communities to use to run QF rounds and drive adoption</li>
<li>Running test rounds with partners to onboard and enable them to manage their own programs permissionlessly</li>
<li>Defining and launching the Core Gitcoin Grants Program in April, to embrace our DAO‚Äôs goal of funding public goods with our protocol</li>
<li>Defining and providing a paid services offering to early adopters until a fully self serve approach is viable</li>
</ol>
<h1><a name="amount-4" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#amount-4"></a>Amount</h1>
<p>PGF‚Äôs total request will be <strong>$606,000 USD</strong> for S17. A 30% decrease from last season. Accounting for 60+ day reserves and current reserves we are requesting <strong>$337,903 USD</strong> for S17. A 68% decrease from last season.</p>
<p><em><strong>1)</strong> The amount of GTC requested and the value of the reserves will be adjusted based on the current market value at the time this proposal is moved to Tally using the lower of the current price or the 20 day moving average, whichever is lower.</em></p>
<p><em><strong>2)</strong> The Treasury Request amount is an estimate using the price of $2.10 GTC to calculate the total of the reserves rolled over from Season 16.</em></p>
