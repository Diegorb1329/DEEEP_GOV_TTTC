**ELECTIONS ARE NOW LIVE**  (post updated 11.28.2022)
As per the steward council v2 proposal, the vote for the next steward council is now live. For details on this process, please see the details below.

As we are using a new quadratic voting strategy, we created a new snapshot site where you will need to join and vote. You can find the vote: https://snapshot.org/#/gitcoingov.eth/proposal/0x5543373d5748b4145b3a8a2cf8ecbf105fcb14d9b136f49134cd51bb4b8f6c75

This vote will be conducted on Snapshot using ‚Äúegalitarian quadratic voting‚Äù which means every registered steward will receive 99 tokens for which they can cast to any number of the 15 candidates using the Quadratic Voting methodology.

Voting period will **close at 12:00 pm UTC on Monday, December 05th**. This is a whitelisted vote, which means this vote is open only to registered Gitcoin Stewards found [here](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-stewards-governance/41/2).

FAQ:
**Q:** What is quadratic voting and how do my votes count?
**A:** See this quick [explainer & simulator](https://www.economist.com/interactive/2021/12/18/quadratic-voting) from The Economist

**Q:** I thought we only vote for 5 days - why is this listed as seven?
**A:** this vote will end over the weekend, which is not the full five ‚Äúwork‚Äù days - so we extend it over the weekend to make sure everyone has plenty of time to vote.

**Q:** Can I change my mind once voted if I see a certain candidate are (not) being upvoted?
**A:** Yes, Votes can be changed while the proposal is active

**Q:** Help - I get the error message ‚ÄúOops, it seems you don't have any voting power at block blahblahblah‚Äú - what does this mean?
**A:** If you are a steward, this means the ETH wallet address you are attempting to vote from is not ‚Äúwhitelisted‚Äù. The steward addresses ‚Äúwhitelisted‚Äù were taken from the [Introducing Steward Governance](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-stewards-governance/41/2) post. 
**A:** If you are no longer using the address you listed as a steward, please contact @shawn on Discord shawn16400#5507 and he can substitute one address with another. If you are not a steward but do want to vote because you hold GTC, this is unfortunately not possible at this time: you can delegate your voting power [here](https://www.daostewards.xyz/) to one of the existing stewards.

**1.Background on the Steward Council:**

In May of 2021 Gitcoin launched GTC, the native governance token for Gitcoin. Along with the creation of GTC, the governance of Gitcoin was handed over to the community via a group of individuals known as Stewards. Stewards are trusted leaders with delegated (including self-delegated) voting power to make decisions on behalf of the community.

To support a move towards more active and engaged governance participation, and recognizing governance took a significant amount of time, the Steward Council was created in March of 2022 and it consisted of the most active and engaged stewards as illustrated by the steward health cards found at [https://www.daostewards.xyz](https://www.daostewards.xyz/)

At the conclusion of v1 of the council, in October 2022, a proposal was made to 1) Update objectives and role of the Steward Council 2. Allocate a monthly stipend to council members and 3) Change the selection process for how steward council members were chosen

Documentation on those efforts can be found here:

* [Introducing the Steward Council](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-the-steward-council/9485)
* [The Steward Council - Formation & Mandate](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/the-steward-council-formation-mandate/9843)
* [Proposal: V2 of the Steward Council](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-v2-of-the-steward-council/11639)

<p><strong>ELECTIONS ARE NOW LIVE</strong>  (post updated 11.28.2022)<br>
As per the steward council v2 proposal, the vote for the next steward council is now live. For details on this process, please see the details below.</p>
<p>As we are using a new quadratic voting strategy, we created a new snapshot site where you will need to join and vote. You can find the vote: <a href="https://snapshot.org/#/gitcoingov.eth/proposal/0x5543373d5748b4145b3a8a2cf8ecbf105fcb14d9b136f49134cd51bb4b8f6c75" class="inline-onebox">Snapshot</a></p>
<p>This vote will be conducted on Snapshot using ‚Äúegalitarian quadratic voting‚Äù which means every registered steward will receive 99 tokens for which they can cast to any number of the 15 candidates using the Quadratic Voting methodology.</p>
<p>Voting period will <strong>close at 12:00 pm UTC on Monday, December 05th</strong>. This is a whitelisted vote, which means this vote is open only to registered Gitcoin Stewards found <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-stewards-governance/41/2">here</a>.</p>
<p>FAQ:<br>
<strong>Q:</strong> What is quadratic voting and how do my votes count?<br>
<strong>A:</strong> See this quick <a href="https://www.economist.com/interactive/2021/12/18/quadratic-voting">explainer &amp; simulator</a> from The Economist</p>
<p><strong>Q:</strong> I thought we only vote for 5 days - why is this listed as seven?<br>
<strong>A:</strong> this vote will end over the weekend, which is not the full five ‚Äúwork‚Äù days - so we extend it over the weekend to make sure everyone has plenty of time to vote.</p>
<p><strong>Q:</strong> Can I change my mind once voted if I see a certain candidate are (not) being upvoted?<br>
<strong>A:</strong> Yes, Votes can be changed while the proposal is active</p>
<p><strong>Q:</strong> Help - I get the error message ‚ÄúOops, it seems you don‚Äôt have any voting power at block blahblahblah‚Äú - what does this mean?<br>
<strong>A:</strong> If you are a steward, this means the ETH wallet address you are attempting to vote from is not ‚Äúwhitelisted‚Äù. The steward addresses ‚Äúwhitelisted‚Äù were taken from the <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-stewards-governance/41/2">Introducing Steward Governance</a> post.<br>
<strong>A:</strong> If you are no longer using the address you listed as a steward, please contact <a class="mention" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/u/shawn">@shawn</a> on Discord shawn16400#5507 and he can substitute one address with another. If you are not a steward but do want to vote because you hold GTC, this is unfortunately not possible at this time: you can delegate your voting power <a href="https://www.daostewards.xyz/">here</a> to one of the existing stewards.</p>

<p><strong>1.Background on the Steward Council:</strong></p>
<p>In May of 2021 Gitcoin launched GTC, the native governance token for Gitcoin. Along with the creation of GTC, the governance of Gitcoin was handed over to the community via a group of individuals known as Stewards. Stewards are trusted leaders with delegated (including self-delegated) voting power to make decisions on behalf of the community.</p>
<p>To support a move towards more active and engaged governance participation, and recognizing governance took a significant amount of time, the Steward Council was created in March of 2022 and it consisted of the most active and engaged stewards as illustrated by the steward health cards found at <a href="https://www.daostewards.xyz/">https://www.daostewards.xyz</a></p>
<p>At the conclusion of v1 of the council, in October 2022, a proposal was made to 1) Update objectives and role of the Steward Council 2. Allocate a monthly stipend to council members and 3) Change the selection process for how steward council members were chosen</p>
<p>Documentation on those efforts can be found here:</p>
<ul>
<li><a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/introducing-the-steward-council/9485">Introducing the Steward Council</a></li>
<li><a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/the-steward-council-formation-mandate/9843">The Steward Council - Formation &amp; Mandate</a></li>
<li><a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-v2-of-the-steward-council/11639">Proposal: V2 of the Steward Council</a></li>
</ul>
