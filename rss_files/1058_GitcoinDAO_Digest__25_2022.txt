### üå± [Public Goods Funding](https://gitcoin.notion.site/Public-Goods-4ccd579f433c4dff9d8952314468d6e8)

* We conducted our GR14 retro ([Miro Board](https://miro.com/app/board/uXjVOpEboic=/), [Notes](https://www.notion.so/gitcoin/GR14-Cross-Functional-Retro-50b9d95e65334419b00c3c446852d551)) and identified key areas we want to improve for GR15. To help us scale, grant owners and reviewers should be provided clearer eligibility critera. Internally, we want better deadlines for each step of the round process: partner engagement, grantee onboarding, and crowdfunding.

* The hype for GR15 is already building! We‚Äôre locking down commitments with our partners, including *drumroll please* Aragon and Loot!

* We rolled out new tooling for Customer Relationship Management (CRM) in Hubspot and are developing fresh documentation.

### [üöÄ](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/proposal-prototyping-workstream-moonshot-budget-request-q4-2021/8785) [Moonshot Collective](https://gitcoin.notion.site/Moonshot-Collective-ffa78f44a0de412cb607f9b69d3a376f)

* **Governance Operations Scaling:** Check out our [demo](https://www.loom.com/share/f055d36f5abe4e65930451e6cde3699a?t=3) for a prototype of what a new governance platform could look like that enables richer, deeper information flows. In this prototype, we‚Äôre showing how enabling voting on multiple options and discourse integration could rocket us forward into a world of greater choice and discussion.

* **Operations:** We‚Äôre protecting ourselves against volatile market conditions by diversifying our treasury into stablecoins. Over 25% of our treasury is now held in DAI.

### üíô [Fraud Detection & Defense (FDD)](https://gitcoin.notion.site/Fraud-Detection-Defense-2bde13c0b8e74fda81435d94e49e2703)

* [GR14 Governance Brief](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/gr14-governance-brief/11050):
  * [**Sybil Detection**](https://hackmd.io/UOlkSVK0QMSEsKEB3fUFjQ?both): We saw a significant increase in Sybil Attacks this round, likely due to [airdrop farming](https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/airdrop-farming/10938), from ~14.1% in GR13 to ~22.1% in GR14. After flagging 16,073 possible Sybils, we narrowed this down to 9,916 likely Sybils and squelched their ability to direct matching funds thereby saving an estimated $879k in Fraud Tax.
  * **Grants Eligibility:** We completed approximately 3,000 reviews of 1,100 grants while reducing our average review cost by 39%. While doing this, we also implemented a new process whereby we review our reviewers to ensure their work is as precise, accurate, and high-quality as possible. From the outset, we announced that high-scoring reviewers could earn a bonus. The results of this process saved an estimated $80,000 in matching funds and cost the DAO $10,260.
  * **Policy Recommendations:** We believe there a number of areas which could benefit from clearer definitions. For example, while we are against outright airdrop farming, we have thus far allowed POAPs, and documenting a clear policy on Quid Pro Quo could make grant reviews easier in future rounds. We should also consider decoupling eligibility in the main grants round from eligibility in side rounds, to prevent any further situations where a side round owner request a grant be admitted that was not eligible for the main round.

### üé® [Merch, Memes & Marketing](https://gitcoin.notion.site/Merch-Memes-and-Marketing-60cc693f0fe14be1879a05587922c8a3)

Our next steps will be to remove the ‚Äòissues‚Äô subfolder from our sitemap and clean up the identified dead links.

* We‚Äôve been developing an internal knowledgebase to streamline our operations and onboarding. We‚Äôre aiming for a soft launch of our [new Notion area](https://level7leadership.notion.site/Welcome-to-Merch-Memes-and-Marketing-566893dda33e4e3abf43a8271dd08175) by the end of the week.

* [**CSDO:**](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCWEiZu1Jqkqn-axtQdtrNLg) At this week‚Äôs CSDO, we‚Äôre keeping our eye on Season 15 (starts August 1st) and setting an intention to plan for it. We‚Äôre also adjusting to the finalization of Asset Transfers from Gitcoin Holdings to the Gitcoin Foundation. * **DAO Tooling**: We‚Äôre sharing a list of [currently used tools within GitcoinDAO](https://gitcoin.notion.site/Tools-used-by-GitcoinDAO-3adf738b63d2415297d5e50a420f7bc6), and performing a review of tool-owners and tool-cost.

<h3>
<a name="fraud-detection-defense-fddhttpsgitcoinnotionsitefraud-detection-defense-2bde13c0b8e74fda81435d94e49e2703-6" class="anchor" href="https://gov.gitcoin.co#fraud-detection-defense-fddhttpsgitcoinnotionsitefraud-detection-defense-2bde13c0b8e74fda81435d94e49e2703-6"></a><img src="https://emoji.discourse-cdn.com/twitter/blue_heart.png?v=12" title=":blue_heart:" class="emoji" alt=":blue_heart:" loading="lazy" width="20" height="20"> <a href="https://gitcoin.notion.site/Fraud-Detection-Defense-2bde13c0b8e74fda81435d94e49e2703" rel="noopener nofollow ugc">Fraud Detection &amp; Defense (FDD)</a>
</h3>
<ul>
<li>
<a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/gr14-governance-brief/11050">GR14 Governance Brief</a>:
<ul>
<li>
<a href="https://hackmd.io/UOlkSVK0QMSEsKEB3fUFjQ?both" rel="noopener nofollow ugc"><strong>Sybil Detection</strong></a>: We saw a significant increase in Sybil Attacks this round, likely due to <a href="https://gov.gitcoin.co/t/airdrop-farming/10938">airdrop farming</a>, from ~14.1% in GR13 to ~22.1% in GR14. After flagging 16,073 possible Sybils, we narrowed this down to 9,916 likely Sybils and squelched their ability to direct matching funds thereby saving an estimated $879k in Fraud Tax.</li>
<li>
<strong>Grants Eligibility:</strong> We completed approximately 3,000 reviews of 1,100 grants while reducing our average review cost by 39%. While doing this, we also implemented a new process whereby we review our reviewers to ensure their work is as precise, accurate, and high-quality as possible. From the outset, we announced that high-scoring reviewers could earn a bonus. The results of this process saved an estimated $80,000 in matching funds and cost the DAO $10,260.</li>
<li>
<strong>Policy Recommendations:</strong> We believe there a number of areas which could benefit from clearer definitions. For example, while we are against outright airdrop farming, we have thus far allowed POAPs, and documenting a clear policy on Quid Pro Quo could make grant reviews easier in future rounds. We should also consider decoupling eligibility in the main grants round from eligibility in side rounds, to prevent any further situations where a side round owner request a grant be admitted that was not eligible for the main round.</li>
</ul>
</li>
</ul>
